# 06 — THE GLOW-UP BRIEF

**Self-directed prompt. Written 2026-07-21. Status: executing.**

---

## The verdict being answered

> "It still has childish features on it. The boxes are all lame, there's not enough pink, there's not enough movement, there's not enough high quality and tons of AI slop, and can we have good text?"

That critique is correct. Naming the specific failures, because "make it better" is not actionable:

| Failure | What it actually is |
|---|---|
| **Lame boxes** | Every component is the same `border-radius: 1.5rem` translucent rectangle. Tracks, places, quotes, contact doors — one shape, repeated eleven times. That is the single most recognizable tell of generated design. |
| **Not enough pink** | Pink appears ~5 times. The restraint rule was applied so hard the brand disappeared. Pink Realty's pink is the asset; currently it is a rumour. |
| **Not enough movement** | One canvas in the footer and some fades. The hero — the most-viewed pixel on the site — is completely static. |
| **AI slop** | Rounded cards in a uniform grid, generic stroke icons, centered closing section, even spacing everywhere. No asymmetry, no editorial structure, no risk. |
| **Bad text** | Section headings are competent and forgettable. Nothing has a point of view. |

---

## The corrective principles

1. **Kill the box.** Cards become editorial objects: hairline rules, real index numbers where sequence is true, asymmetric spans, flush-left structure. Radius is earned, not default. Not one uniform grid on the page.
2. **Pink becomes structural light.** Section blooms, pink hairlines, pink indices, pink glow on the live figure. Still never a flat pink wall — but present in every section instead of five times total.
3. **Movement is continuous, not incidental.** Scroll-scrub the hero. Parallax the Atlas. Terrain in the footer. Counters, marquee, press bloom. Something is always alive without anything ever being noisy.
4. **Text gets a point of view.** Every heading makes a claim someone could disagree with. No "People I've worked with."
5. **Asymmetry.** Nothing centered unless centering is the point.

---

## Scope, in build order

1. **Hero → Garden of the Gods**, scroll-scrubbed: image scales and drifts, copy parallaxes, scrim deepens as you leave.
2. **Atlas → a real journey.** Drawn from *actual Colorado Springs coordinates* — every area, Pikes Peak, downtown, and the four installations positioned truthfully. Scroll-driven pan.
3. **Editorial card system** replacing all uniform boxes.
4. **Persistent side call rail** — desktop, always reachable, ELP-appropriate language.
5. **Market data strip** — real numbers, dated, owner-updated. No fake live feed.
6. **Signature wordmark + corner portrait.**
7. **Copy pass** on every heading.

---

## Two things this brief refuses

**No forged signature.** A signature is an identity artifact; drawing one for a real person and presenting it as his handwriting is forgery, regardless of intent. What ships instead is an openly *designed script wordmark* — a logotype, labelled as such in the code, with a note to swap in a real scan. It gives the same warmth without pretending to be his hand.

**No stock or scraped neighborhood photos.** Captioning a downloaded photo "Briargate" when it is not Briargate is misrepresentation, and neighborhood imagery is Fair Housing territory (`04_COMPLIANCE.md` §3). The Atlas is therefore built from **true geography** — real coordinates, real distances, real construction eras — which is more honest *and* more distinctive than stock photography would have been. Real neighborhood photos slot in later when Jake shoots them.
